# 📊 Findings

## 📖 Overview

This document summarizes the major findings derived from the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) conducted on construction contractor data collected from Madurai, Tamil Nadu.

The study aimed to evaluate contractor performance, analyze business characteristics, identify geographical patterns, and investigate factors influencing housing affordability. Through data cleaning, statistical analysis, visualization, and correlation assessment, several meaningful insights were uncovered regarding contractor operations and market dynamics.

The findings provide valuable guidance for homeowners, builders, researchers, investors, and policymakers seeking to make informed decisions within the construction sector.

---

# 🎯 Research Outcomes

The analysis successfully achieved the project's primary objectives by:

* Identifying patterns in contractor performance.
* Evaluating relationships between business characteristics and success indicators.
* Understanding geographical trends in contractor distribution.
* Assessing factors affecting housing affordability.
* Supporting evidence-based contractor selection.

---

# ⭐ Customer Rating Analysis

Customer ratings emerged as one of the strongest indicators of contractor performance.

## Key Findings

### High Overall Ratings

The majority of contractors in the dataset maintained ratings above 4.5.

### Average Rating

The average contractor rating was approximately:

```text
4.77 / 5.00
```

### Interpretation

This suggests that many contractors operating in Madurai maintain high service quality and strong customer satisfaction levels.

---

## Impact of Ratings

Highly rated contractors typically demonstrated:

* Better customer trust
* Stronger market reputation
* Greater business stability
* Higher client retention rates

---

# 💰 Annual Turnover Analysis

Annual turnover was analyzed to understand financial performance across contractors.

## Key Findings

### Strong Revenue Variability

Turnover varied significantly across contractors due to differences in:

* Workforce size
* Project volume
* Service offerings
* Business maturity

---

### High-Performing Contractors

Several contractors demonstrated strong financial performance despite operating with relatively small teams.

### Interpretation

Business efficiency and specialization can contribute significantly to financial success.

---

# 👷 Workforce Analysis

Employee count was examined as a measure of organizational capacity.

## Key Findings

### Larger Firms

Contractors with:

```text
19–38 Employees
```

generally reported:

* Higher turnover
* Greater project capacity
* Larger client portfolios

---

### Smaller Firms

Smaller organizations frequently demonstrated:

* Operational flexibility
* Faster decision-making
* Competitive service quality

---

## Interpretation

Workforce size positively influences business capacity, but efficiency and expertise remain equally important.

# 📈 Correlation Findings

Several relationships were identified through correlation analysis.

---

## Positive Relationships

### Ratings and Turnover

Higher customer ratings were generally associated with stronger financial performance.

---

### Employee Count and Turnover

Contractors with larger workforces often reported higher annual turnover.

---

### Service Diversity and Ratings

Offering a wider range of services positively influenced customer satisfaction.

---

## Weak Relationships

### Company Age and Ratings

Business age showed limited influence on customer ratings.

---

### Company Age and Turnover

Older firms did not necessarily generate higher revenues than newer firms.

---

## Interpretation

Business success depends more on service quality, operational efficiency, and customer satisfaction than on age alone.

# 🏠 Housing Affordability Insights

One of the primary goals of this study was to examine factors influencing housing affordability.

---

## Key Findings

### Contractor Availability Matters

Regions with higher contractor density often provide:

* Greater competition
* Better pricing options
* More service choices

---

### Service Diversity Reduces Complexity

Contractors offering end-to-end services reduce the need for multiple vendors, helping homeowners save time and potentially reduce costs.

---

### Experienced Contractors Improve Project Outcomes

Although age alone does not determine success, experienced contractors often demonstrate:

* Better project management
* Improved planning
* Reduced construction risks

---

## Interpretation

Affordable housing is influenced not only by material costs but also by contractor efficiency, competition, and service quality.
