# 🏗️ Methodology

## 📖 Overview

This research adopts a data-driven methodology to evaluate contractor performance and housing affordability factors in Madurai, Tamil Nadu. The study integrates primary and secondary data sources, applies Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) techniques, and utilizes statistical visualization methods to identify patterns, relationships, and trends within the construction industry.

The methodology was designed to support informed decision-making by homeowners, researchers, policymakers, and construction professionals through systematic analysis of contractor characteristics, operational capabilities, and business performance indicators.

The overall research process consists of five major stages:

1. Data Collection
2. Data Validation and Verification
3. Data Preprocessing
4. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
5. Visualization and Interpretation

---

# 📥 Data Collection

The dataset was developed through a combination of primary and secondary data collection techniques.

The objective was to compile a comprehensive database of construction contractors operating in Madurai and surrounding regions.

---

## 🌐 Secondary Data Collection

Initial contractor information was gathered from publicly accessible sources.

### Sources Used

* Justdial
* Sulekha
* Houzz
* Contractor Websites
* Google Business Listings
* Social Media Business Pages
* Industry Directories

### Information Collected

* Company Name
* Address
* Contact Information
* Business Type
* Ratings
* Specializations
* Service Offerings

---

## 📞 Primary Data Collection

To improve dataset accuracy and completeness, contractors were contacted directly through telephone interviews.

### Information Verified

* Annual Turnover
* Employee Strength
* Nature of Business
* Years of Operation
* Project Categories
* Special Services Offered

### Purpose

Direct communication ensured:

* Improved data reliability
* Reduced misinformation
* Better understanding of contractor operations

---

## 📄 Supporting Sources

Additional information was collected from:

### Government Publications

* Tamil Nadu Department of Economics and Statistics
* Housing and Urban Development Reports
* Infrastructure Development Reports

### Industry Reports

* Construction Market Studies
* Real Estate Publications
* Housing Affordability Reports

### News Sources

Industry-related news articles and construction incident reports were reviewed to understand local construction challenges and trends.

# ✅ Data Validation

Before analysis, all collected information underwent validation procedures.

### Verification Methods

* Cross-referencing contractor information across multiple platforms
* Comparing ratings from different review sources
* Validating business details through company websites
* Confirming contact information

### Benefits

* Improved dataset accuracy
* Reduced duplicate entries
* Enhanced reliability of findings

# 🧹 Data Preprocessing

Raw contractor data often contains inconsistencies that must be addressed before meaningful analysis can be performed.

Several preprocessing techniques were applied.

---

## Missing Value Handling

Records containing incomplete information were reviewed.

### Techniques Applied

* Missing value identification
* Data correction where possible
* Removal of unusable records

---

## Duplicate Removal

Duplicate contractor entries were identified and removed.

### Purpose

* Prevent data redundancy
* Improve analytical accuracy
* Ensure unique contractor representation

---

## Data Standardization

Variables were standardized to maintain consistency.

### Examples

* Uniform naming conventions
* Standardized rating formats
* Consistent financial representation
* Categorized business types

---

## Data Transformation

Several attributes were transformed into analysis-ready formats.

### Examples

* Ratings converted to numerical values
* Founded year converted to business age
* Turnover grouped into categories
* Employee counts categorized by company size
